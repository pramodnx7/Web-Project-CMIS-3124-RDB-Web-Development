Customer Charter of Licensed Banks

Introduction

This Charter sets key standards of fair banking practices envisaged by customers when they undertake transactions with licensed banks and provides guidance to the licensed banks to adopt a 'Code of Conduct' on customer protection.

The Charter also includes a set of customer obligations towards licensed banks in the interest of stable relationship.

This charter is supervised by the Central Bank of Sri Lanka

Receiving Information & Understanding Products/Services

The customers have the right to receive factual information and understand the financial products/services offered by banks. Good practices include:

Key Facts Document

Each licensed bank should have a Key Facts Document in the form of a brochure/leaflet written in simple language, available in Sinhala/Tamil/English, containing:

  • Description of the products/services
  • Financial and other benefits to customers including incentives and promotions
  • Fees/charges, commission, interest etc., charged from customers
  • Procedures to be followed to obtain the product/service
  • Major terms and conditions
  • A common complaint procedure for customers

Advertisements

All advertisements by licensed banks should:

  • Contain factual information on products/services
  • Be understood by the targeted customers
  • Not contain information that may mislead the public
  • Give contact details and state the bank is supervised by the Central Bank of Sri Lanka

Information Display Requirements

The following should be displayed in Head Offices and all branches:

  • Current interest rates on all deposit and loan products
  • Buying and selling rates of foreign currencies
  • Credit rating of the bank with underlying specifications
  • Contact details of the Financial Ombudsman and Credit Counselling Centre
  • Banking hours and Holiday notices
  • Any other relevant information

Credit Card Statements

Statements for credit cards should set out:

  • The minimum payment required
  • Total interest amount charged if only minimum payment is made
  • Late payment fee if minimum payment is not made

Awareness & Understanding Terms & Conditions

Customers have the right to access and fully understand the terms and conditions relevant to each product or service.

Bank Obligations

Licensed banks should ensure that:

  • Terms and Conditions are available in languages preferred by customers
  • A copy is given to the prospective customer prior to offering a product
  • Relationship officers clearly explain terms, conditions and features
  • Comparison of alternative products/services is provided
  • Reasonable time is given for customers to make decisions
  • Written confirmation is obtained that details were received and understood
  • All documents are duly completed and signed (no blank papers)
  • Changes to agreed terms are informed in writing before implementation

Customer Rights Under Terms & Conditions

Customers have the right to know:

  • Bank's general charges including interest rates, fees, commissions and computation methods
  • Bank's complaint receiving procedure and resolution mechanism
  • Recovery actions in case of customer default and reimbursable expenses
  • Compensation for pre-mature withdrawal/termination
  • Restrictions on opening, closing, maintaining accounts and dormant account policies
  • Disclosure of customer information to legally authorised parties
  • Rules regarding suspicious transaction reporting to Financial Intelligence Unit
  • Reporting procedures for stolen cards/financial instruments
  • Foreclosure procedures on collateral property

Compensation from Withdrawal/Cancellation

Your Rights

In the event a licensed bank seeks to withdraw/terminate a product or service already on contract, especially deposit products, customers have the right to receive:

  • Reasonable time to make alternative arrangements
  • Exit compensation scheme disclosed in advance

Protection from Agents of Banks

Customers have the right to know the details of agents appointed for customer services and the 'Code of Conduct' issued to them by banks.

Agents are Prohibited From:

Harassing customers
Using abusive debt collection practices
Disclosing customer information to others
Giving false or misleading information
Unduly influencing customers to buy products
Getting security documents signed outside the bank

Complaint Measures and Relief

Customers have the right to resolve their complaints with transparency and effectively.

Bank Requirements

Licensed banks should:

  • Implement a quick and effective resolution mechanism
  • Rectify disputes quickly and handle complaints within a short period
  • Direct customers to take complaints forward if still not satisfied
  • Reverse any charges that applied due to a mistake
  • Have a written procedure for receiving and resolving complaints
  • Acknowledge receipt of complaints in writing within a reasonably short period
  • Inform complainants of the resolution procedure and contact details
  • Accept complaints verbally or in writing (not only in writing)
  • Establish a management information system for complaints
  • Assign an officer for handling complaints in each branch
  • Advise customers to seek affordable and efficient resolution

Special Attention and Care

We Care for Everyone

The following customers have the right to receive special attention to facilitate fair access to banking services:

Elderly Customers
Disabled Customers
Customers with Low Financial Literacy

Customer Obligations Toward Banks

Customers should foster the relationship with banks by fulfilling their obligations:

1

Do not borrow beyond your affordable repayment capacity limit

2

Do not allow repayments or instalments to go into arrears - prompt repayments create healthy relationships

3

Early loan settlement may require paying an additional amount as agreed at the time of accepting the offer

4

If unable to repay, the bank has the right to recover amounts owing including expenses specified in Terms & Conditions

5

If in financial difficulties, inform the bank as early as possible for easier solutions

6

Always inform the bank of any changes to your address and contact details

7

Have full knowledge and understanding of products/services before entering contracts

8

Duly fill and submit required application forms and supporting documents on time

9

Exercise due care in all transactions with banks

10

Notify the bank promptly of any fraudulent transactions or attempts in your accounts

11

Exercise utmost care in using and storing PINs and security measures of electronic cards

12

Do not treat operational lapses as reason for non-settlement of debt unless allowed by a court of law
